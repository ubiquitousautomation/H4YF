## 03 Q47 — Air Jordan 1 authentication tells

**Q:** How do I tell if Air Jordan 1s are fake?

**A:** Jordan 1 fakes have gotten extremely sophisticated, but here are the consistent tells I look for: the wing logo stitching count (authentic pairs have precise stitch counts that fakes often miss), the shape of the toe box profile (fakes tend to run too flat or too round depending on the factory), the texture and rigidity of the ankle collar padding, the Jumpman angle on the heel counter (proportions are a giveaway), the midsole edge color and finish, and the Nike Air branding on the insole font. Box label matching — size, style code, and colorway — is also critical. I can go deeper by colorway and year. For a specific pair you're questioning, book the $45 auth at heat4yafeat.com — I won't call a pair real or fake on a description alone.

## 03 Q48 — Air Jordan 11 authentication

**Q:** How do I authenticate Air Jordan 11s?

**A:** Jordan 11s are one of the most counterfeited silhouettes because they're so iconic. Key tells: the carbon fiber plate on the outsole — fakes often have a different weave pattern, less depth, or wrong sheen. The patent leather overlay quality is a major checkpoint — authentic pairs have a specific weight and flexibility, and the seam placement along the mudguard is precise. The midsole pods on the bottom have a specific shape and alignment. The lace locks (the toggle hardware) are often wrong on fakes. Tongue embroidery alignment matters too. No two fakes get all of these right simultaneously — that's where experience beats a checklist. For a specific pair, book the $45 auth at heat4yafeat.com.

## 06 Q144 — How to store and preserve sneakers

**Q:** How should I store my sneakers to preserve them?

**A:** Cool, dry, dark storage is the baseline — heat, humidity, and UV light are the enemies of sneaker materials. Original boxes are best for protection and maintaining collector presentation. For long-term storage, many collectors use individual plastic sneaker containers (The Container Store and similar carry them). Crumple paper in the toe box to maintain shape during storage. Cedar inserts help with any moisture. For materials like patent leather (Jordan 11), keep them away from anything that can crease them in storage. Most importantly: check on stored pairs periodically — the "crumble" phenomenon (sole separation on old pairs) can progress even in storage. I touch on this in @HEAT4YAFEAT YouTube content.

## 06 Q145 — What is "sole crumble" and how to prevent it

**Q:** What is sole crumble and how do I avoid it?

**A:** Sole crumble (also called sole rot or foam degradation) is the breakdown of the polyurethane foam used in midsoles, particularly on pairs from the late 80s through 90s. The material breaks down over time regardless of whether the shoe is worn — and often accelerates in high humidity or with temperature fluctuations. Prevention: cool, dry, dark storage; avoid extreme temperature swings; some collectors apply products designed to slow the process. Restoration: sole swap services exist for pairs where the outsole can be replaced while preserving the upper. For vintage pairs at risk, this is a real concern. I address it in the @HEAT4YAFEAT YouTube channel.
