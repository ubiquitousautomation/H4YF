## Fee Comparison (Single Source of Truth)

Before setting any floor price, know what each platform costs you.

| Platform | Seller Fee | Payment Processing | Shipping (avg $12 box) | Effective Take Rate |
|----------|-----------|--------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| StockX | 9.5% + 3% (new sellers) | Included | Seller pays | ~15–17% |
| eBay | 12.9% (shoes >$150 exempt to 8%) | 2.9% + $0.30 | Seller pays | ~12–16% |
| Whatnot | 8% | $0.30/transaction | Buyer pays (Whatnot label) | ~8.5% flat |

**Whatnot advantage:** Buyer pays shipping. This is a 5–8% margin gain on a $150 shoe vs. eBay where you eat the label. Factor this into every floor price calculation.

## Whatnot-Specific Pricing Principles

### 1. Start Lower Than Feels Comfortable

The #1 mistake new Whatnot sellers make is treating starting bid like a eBay BIN price. They're not the same.

A $1 start on a shoe with a $500 market value will almost always close higher than a $350 start — because:
- Low starts create urgency and FOMO
- Multiple bidders competing drives emotional pricing
- The algorithm surfaces auctions with active bids

**Rule:** Start at $1 on anything with 3+ potential bidders in the room. Start at 50% of market on rare/obscure items where demand is unclear.

### 2. Set a Hidden Reserve, Not a High Start

If you're uncomfortable with $1 starts (understandable for high-value items), use a reserve price rather than a high starting bid. Reserve keeps the energy of a low start while protecting your floor.

Platforms that support reserves: Whatnot supports reserve pricing in the lot setup.

### 3. The $1 Blitz Show

Once per month: run a full show where every lot starts at $1, no exceptions. Announce it in advance. This show will:
- Pull in viewers who normally don't tune in
- Drive your highest GMV show of the month
- Generate clip-worthy moments for social content

The items that close low will be offset by rooms full of engaged bidders driving everything else up.

### 4. Lot Bundling for Margin Improvement

Dead inventory that won't sell individually? Bundle it.

- 2 pairs same colorway, different sizes → "Size run bundle"
- 3 lower-demand pairs with complementary brand story → "New Balance deep cut lot"
- Accessories + budget pair → "Starter kit"

Bundling moves slow inventory, keeps show pace high, and often yields better per-unit margin than individual listings.

### 5. Price Tiers by Show Segment

Structure each show so price points escalate:

| Segment | Target Price Range | Strategy |
|---------|-------------------|---------|
| Openers (first 10 lots) | $25–$75 | $1 start, fast close, build room energy |
| Mid-tier block 1 | $75–$200 | $1 start or 20% of market, tell the story |
| Mid-tier block 2 | $200–$400 | Reserve + $1 start, allow full clock |
| Anchor close | $400+ | No reserve on one, reserve on others, maximize energy |

## Cross-Platform Pricing Discipline

You're selling on eBay, StockX, AND Whatnot simultaneously. Rules to avoid arbitrage problems:

1. **Whatnot goes live first for new arrivals.** Exclusivity builds the audience. List on eBay after the show.
2. **Never price Whatnot above StockX** for the same item — you'll lose the room. Price at or below market to drive bids.
3. **Pull eBay listings before a show** for anything in your lot. Avoid selling the same item twice.
4. **After a show:** Items that didn't sell can immediately go to eBay at market price. No discount needed — the auction audience has already told you the market.

## Inventory Tiering System

Classify every item when it enters inventory:

| Tier | Description | Whatnot Strategy |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **S-Tier** | Grails, collabs, high-demand — instant sell on any platform | Hold for anchor show slots |
| **A-Tier** | Clean retros, broad size appeal, $150–$400 market | Mid-tier show staple, $1 start |
| **B-Tier** | Good shoes, limited demand, slow movers on StockX | Bundle, $1 start, no reserve |
| **C-Tier** | Aged inventory, off-sizes, condition issues | Opener fodder or bundle filler |

Never put C-tier in your first 5 shows. Build reputation first.

## Cashflow Notes

- Whatnot pays out **T+3 business days** after the show ends
- eBay: 2–3 business days post-ship
- StockX: 3–7 business days post-authentication

Whatnot payout timing is competitive. Factor this into show frequency decisions — cash redeployment into new inventory is faster than StockX.
