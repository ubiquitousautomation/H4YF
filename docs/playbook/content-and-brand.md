## Show Persona

The show persona is the brand's face. It needs to be consistent, recognizable, and repeatable.

### Core Identity Pillars

1. **The Expert Plug** — You know every colorway, every release history, every tell on a fake. This is the credibility pillar. Never faked, always authenticated.
2. **The Entertainer** — You make the room fun. Not a comedian — an energetic host who makes shoe talk interesting even for casual viewers.
3. **The Community Builder** — You remember names. You ask questions. You make buyers feel like they're in a circle, not a transaction.

### On-Stream Behaviors That Reinforce the Brand

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Call followers by username | Read the chat robotically |
| Tell the story behind each shoe | Just list specs (size, color, DS) |
| Admit when you don't know something | Fake authority |
| React genuinely to high bids | Fake hype/manufactured urgency |
| Move fast between lots | Let dead air sit more than 10 seconds |
| Authenticate on camera (show details, tags) | Skip authentication for speed |

### Catchphrases / Show Rituals (Pick 2–3, Use Every Show)

Consistency builds ritual. When regulars can predict what you'll say, they feel like insiders.

Examples to build around:
- **Show open:** "Welcome back to HEAT4YAFEAT — the only place you need to be for authentic sneaker heat."
- **Before a grail lot:** "Y'all ready? This is what we saved for."
- **After a sale:** "Winner winner, fresh sneaker dinner. [Username], these are on their way to you."
- **Follow ask:** "Hit that follow button — it's how you stay in the loop."

## Visual Identity

### Profile & Banner

- **Color palette:** Black, white, and a single accent (fire orange #FF4500 or electric blue #00C3FF work well for sneaker brands)
- **Profile photo:** High-quality logo or clean brand mark. If personal brand: well-lit headshot with branded background.
- **Show banner:** Clean graphic with brand name, "LIVE SNEAKER AUCTIONS", and a thumbnail of heat inventory. Update seasonally or when you have a new grail in stock.

### On-Camera Setup Aesthetic

Your setup is part of the brand. It should say "serious seller" on first impression.

| Element | Goal | Implementation |
|---------|------|---------------|
| Background | Credibility + aspiration | Shoe wall, branded pegboard, or clean neutral with logo |
| Lighting | Professional quality | Ring light (front) or two soft boxes at 45° |
| Surface | Clean product display | Black velvet mat or white acrylic display board |
| Wardrobe | Cohesive with brand | Clean fits — show the sneaker game you sell. Wear heat. |
| Text overlays | Reinforce brand during stream | Whatnot supports overlays — add brand name + social handles |

## Content Pillars (Social + Clips)

Every piece of content you post outside Whatnot should map to one of these pillars:

### 1. Authentication Content
**Format:** Short video showing how you authenticate
**Why it works:** Builds trust, differentiates from resellers who don't authenticate, drives search traffic
**Examples:**
- "How to spot a fake Jordan 1 in 30 seconds"
- "3 places to check before you buy vintage Nike"
- "I almost bought a fake — here's what saved me"

### 2. The Flip/Score Story
**Format:** Before/after reveal — bought for $X, worth $Y
**Why it works:** Aspirational + entertainment value. Everyone loves a come-up story.
**Examples:**
- "Found these at an estate sale for $40"
- "Thrift store find just went for $[price] on my Whatnot show"
- "Bought the wrong size, turned a $20 profit anyway"

### 3. Show Highlights
**Format:** Clip auction closes, big bids, reactions
**Why it works:** Social proof + entertainment. Converts viewers who weren't in the room.
**Examples:**
- "[Shoe] went from $1 to $[final] in 60 seconds"
- "The room went crazy when I revealed this pair"
- "Chat vs. buyer — the bidding war on this Jordan 1"

### 4. Education
**Format:** Explainer videos, history of a shoe, breakdown of a release
**Why it works:** Builds expertise positioning. SEO-friendly on TikTok/YouTube.
**Examples:**
- "Why the 2015 Jordan 1 Chicago is the most important sneaker of the decade"
- "Breaking down every Nike SB Dunk collaboration, ranked"
- "The 5 most faked sneakers on Whatnot right now"

### 5. Behind the Scenes
**Format:** Show prep, inventory arrival, packing orders
**Why it works:** Humanizes the brand. Makes buyers feel like insiders.
**Examples:**
- "Prepping 40 pairs for tonight's show"
- "Just got a collection off Craigslist — let's see what's in the bag"
- "How I pack to make sure your pair arrives perfect"

## Show Title Formula

Whatnot titles are searchable. Optimize them.

**Structure:** `[Brand Name] | [Hero Item] + [Category] | [Format] + [Hook]`

**Examples:**
- `HEAT4YAFEAT | Jordan 1s & Dunks | $1 Starts 🔥 | Ships Same Day`
- `H4YF LIVE | Off-White Collabs + DS Jordans | Authenticated Heat`
- `HEAT4YAFEAT | $1 Blitz Night — Everything Starts at $1`
- `H4YF | Streetwear + Sneakers | Grail Alert Closing Tonight`

Always include: brand name, at least one recognizable product, and either a format hook ($1 starts) or urgency signal (tonight, grail, last pair).

## Authenticity as Competitive Advantage

The sneaker resale market has an authenticity problem. Fakes are getting better. Buyers are increasingly skeptical. Your advantage is demonstrating the authentication process live, on camera, every show.

Build a repeatable on-camera authentication ritual:
1. Show the box — legit boxes have specific features by year
2. Show the tongue tag — font, spacing, stitching
3. Show the sole — midsole quality, pattern alignment
4. Show the insole — Nike/Jordan branding placement
5. Hold up to camera at detail level

This 60-second ritual builds more trust than any marketing copy. Do it for every pair that warrants it (DS, high-value items). Buyers will tell their sneaker friends: "This show actually shows you the shoe before you bid."

That word-of-mouth is your best growth channel.
