# Whatnot Launch Strategy — HEAT4YAFEAT

## Mission

Move H4YF from passive listing platforms (eBay, StockX) into a live, community-driven channel where authentic sneaker knowledge converts browsers into loyal buyers and repeat customers.

## The Core Opportunity

Whatnot isn't just another marketplace — it's a performance. Buyers come for the seller as much as the product. An established inventory base + existing authentication expertise is a massive head start that most new Whatnot sellers don't have. The goal is to translate that into a must-watch show, not just a marketplace listing.

---

## Phase 0 — Foundation (Days 1–14)

### Account Setup

- [ ] Create Whatnot seller account at whatnot.com/sell
- [ ] Submit seller verification (ID + bank account)
- [ ] Apply for Sneakers category approval (required before going live)
- [ ] Set up payout method (ACH — fastest settlement)

### Profile Build-Out

- [ ] **Profile photo:** Clean logo or personal brand photo — avoid blank avatars
- [ ] **Bio:** Lead with category + credibility: "Sneakers & streetwear plug — authenticated heat, established eBay seller, ships same day." Include IG handle.
- [ ] **Banner:** High-quality image — shoe wall, flat lay of upcoming inventory, or brand graphic
- [ ] **Shipping profile:** Set default to USPS Priority (2–3 day) — Whatnot buyers expect fast shipping
- [ ] **Return policy:** No returns on live show items — state clearly in bio and at show open

### Cross-Platform Bridge

This is the highest-leverage move for an established seller: your existing buyers on eBay and StockX don't know you're on Whatnot yet. Bridge that gap before the first show.

- [ ] Add Whatnot profile link to all active eBay listings (in item description)
- [ ] Update eBay bio/About section to mention Whatnot
- [ ] Post "We're going live" announcement on any social channels
- [ ] Create IG/TikTok teaser: inventory preview + launch date countdown

### Inventory Prep for Launch

Select 40–60 SKUs for the launch show. Prioritize:

1. **Anchor pieces (5–8 items):** High-recognizability grails/collabs that drive tune-ins. Examples: Jordan 1 Retro High, Nike SB Dunks, Off-White collabs. Don't start your show — open with these as the closer.
2. **Mid-tier movers (20–30 items):** Clean, in-demand retros with broad size appeal. GR (general release) Jordans, NB 990s, clean Adidas. These are your bread and butter.
3. **Entry-point openers (10–15 items):** Sub-$75 heat — budget Dunks, collab accessories, streetwear accessories. Win early bidders, build the room energy.

Physical prep for every item:
- [ ] Photograph box, lace swap if needed, authenticator tags
- [ ] Note size, colorway, condition code (DS, VNDS, used)
- [ ] Pull comps from StockX + GOAT for each pair — know your floor before you go live
- [ ] Organize by show order (openers → mid-tier → anchors)

## Phase 1 — Soft Launch (Days 15–45)

### Show Cadence

Start with **2 shows per week** — not more. Consistency matters more than frequency at launch. Overextending inventory or energy kills momentum.

Recommended schedule:
- **Thursday 7–9 PM local time** (prime mid-week slot, light competition)
- **Saturday 12–3 PM local time** (high-traffic Whatnot window)

### First Show Blueprint

Duration: **90 minutes max**. Fatigue sets in fast on both sides.

| Segment | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Warm-up | 10 min | Greet newcomers, show the setup, build the room |
| Openers | 20 min | 8–10 low-ticket items — fast pace, get bids moving |
| Mid-tier block 1 | 25 min | 6–8 pairs, tell the story of each shoe |
| Break / Community | 5 min | Shout followers, tease anchors |
| Mid-tier block 2 | 20 min | Another 6–8 pairs |
| Anchor close | 10 min | 2–3 grails — max energy, saved for the room peak |

### Show Performance Rules

1. **Never dead air.** If a lot isn't getting bids, narrate: colorway history, how you authenticate, who you'd style it with.
2. **Call out new followers by name.** This is the single highest-ROI action in a Whatnot show.
3. **Authenticity is your edge.** You know these shoes. Talk about why this pair matters — release date story, collab backstory, who's worn it. eBay listings can't do this.
4. **Start prices low.** It feels wrong. Do it anyway. $1 starts on mid-tier items create bidding wars. $25 starts kill rooms. You can always floor items pre-show if they don't sell.
5. **Never fake urgency.** Buyers can smell scripted hype. Genuine knowledge > manufactured pressure.

### Show Tech Setup

- **Camera:** Minimum 1080p — iPhone 12+ in landscape works. Dedicated webcam or Sony ZV-1 is better.
- **Lighting:** Ring light OR two softboxes angled at 45°. Kill overhead fluorescents.
- **Background:** Shoe wall, branded backdrop, or clean neutral. No clutter behind you.
- **Audio:** Wired earbuds or USB mic (Blue Snowball, Rode NT-USB). Built-in laptop mic is a disqualifier.
- **Internet:** Wired ethernet if possible. Min 10 Mbps upload. Test before every show.
- **Display:** Second monitor or tablet to watch the chat in real time.
- **Table:** Clean surface at camera height for shoe displays. Black velvet mat reads well on video.

## Phase 2 — Growth Mode (Days 46–90)

### Scaling Show Frequency

By Week 6, move to **3 shows per week** if:
- Average concurrent viewers > 25 in Phase 1
- Sell-through rate > 70% per show
- Post-show followers are growing week-over-week

Add a **Tuesday drop show** (themed or new arrivals) as the third slot.

### Show Formats to Rotate

| Format | Description | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Standard Auction** | Open bids, highest wins | Default — every show |
| **Fixed Price Drop** | Flat price, first claim wins | New release day drops |
| **Mystery Box** | Bundled lot, revealed live | High-energy special events |
| **Countdown Clock** | Time-limited bid window | Anchor pieces / grails |
| **$1 Start Blitz** | Everything starts at $1 | Engagement-builder shows |

### Whatnot Algorithm Levers

Whatnot surfaces sellers in the feed based on:
1. **Follower count** — actively ask viewers to follow during every show
2. **Show frequency** — consistent schedule is weighted positively
3. **Completion rate** — never cancel a scheduled show
4. **Buyer ratings** — ship fast, communicate proactively
5. **GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)** — higher-value shows rank better

The ask: "If you're not following yet, hit that follow button — it's how you know when we go live and it helps the show." Say it at open, at the break, and at close. Every show.

### Positioning vs. Competing Whatnot Sneaker Shows

Study the top 5 sneaker sellers on Whatnot:
- Note their show times (don't overlap if possible)
- Note their price range (find your lane)
- Note what they *don't* carry — that's your opportunity
- Never trash-talk competitors on stream

H4YF differentiator to lean into: **Curation + authentication story.** Not a flip house, not a bulk liquidator — authenticated heat with a narrative. Buyers pay more for that.

## Phase 3 — Channel Maturity (Days 91–180)

### Revenue Mix Target

| Channel | Month 1 | Month 3 | Month 6 |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| eBay | 70% | 50% | 35% |
| StockX | 20% | 15% | 10% |
| Whatnot | 10% | 35% | 55% |

### Whatnot Seller Tier Milestones

| Tier | What Unlocks |
|------|-------------|
| **Rising Star** | Featured placement, priority support |
| **Top Seller** | Homepage feature, reduced fees (negotiate), brand partnership access |
| **Power Seller** | Whatnot marketing budget, exclusive early access events |

Reach Top Seller by month 3. Requirements vary — target: 100+ completed shows, 4.9+ rating, $50K+ GMV.

### Community Monetization Beyond Shows

- **Whatnot Giveaways:** Run a follow + share giveaway on IG, send winner a low-cost pair. Net follower cost < $0.50 each.
- **Pre-Show Presales:** List select items as fixed-price "pre-show" picks for followers 24h before going live.
- **Subscriber Exclusives:** Offer first pick on new arrivals to Whatnot followers before eBay listing.
- **Collab Shows:** Partner with a complementary seller (streetwear, accessories, hats) for a co-hosted show. Both audiences merge.

## Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Low viewership in first shows | High | Pre-promote on IG/TikTok, schedule during peak times, have supporters join and bid early |
| Inventory mismatch (no interest in what you listed) | Medium | Run comps before every show, keep $1 starts available |
| Shipping delays damaging rating | Medium | Ship within 24h, proactively message buyers on delays |
| Authentication dispute | Low | Document every item with photos before shipping, use reputable auth services |
| Platform fee changes | Low | Monitor Whatnot seller terms; diversify revenue across platforms |
| Burnout from show prep | Medium | Batch photography sessions, build show templates, hire a PA at scale |
